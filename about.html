About

Nika Grigorian is an Berlin based interdisciplinary artist, curator and researcher. At the core of her practice is the method of field research. By seeking out places, events and people that remain hidden from general view, she seeks to liberate knowledge from a one-dimensional narrative and create exchange.

Her experiences as a journalist and director of an art magazine shape her methodological framework. In 2020, Grigorian digitised the University of the Arts' student magazine "eigenart". In doing so, she created a community platform to present artistic discussions and take them beyond the academic framework.

In 2021, Grigorian completed her studies at the Institute "Kunst Im Kontext" with her work "Kokvitskaya Gora - the process of standing for community rights", in which she dealt with questions surrounding the sustainable development of isolated village structures in the Komi Republic (northern Russia). She developed a proposal of the inhabitants for a political manifesto as the collective voice of a community that wants to be heard.

Born and raised in St Petersburg, Russia, she is the child of an Armenian father and a Komi mother. She spent her childhood between the urban space of St.Petersburg and the Komi countryside, which influenced her artistic visions.
